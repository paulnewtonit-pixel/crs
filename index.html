New evidence-led analysis

Section 25 Analysis

Upload the applicant’s and respondent’s Section 25 statements to produce separate provisional party analyses and a neutral comparative matrix. CourtReady organises each stated position against the statutory factors, links financial resources and needs to the Form E analysis, preserves page references, and highlights disputed assertions, missing support and points requiring review—without predicting the court’s decision.

New cross-document analysis

Evidence Consistency Analysis

Upload supporting evidence for either party and compare it with the Forms E and Section 25 statements already analysed. CourtReady identifies matching information, discrepancies, missing support and potentially stale or unclear material, then produces a page-referenced disclosure checklist and prioritised review points—without making findings of fact or dishonesty.

How it works

Five clear steps from first account to managed case record.

Information remains reviewable throughout. Nothing identified in the Investigation workspace enters the permanent case record without approval.

  1. 01

    Select or create your case

    Sign in, choose an existing matter or deliberately create a new case.

  2. 02

    Tell CourtReady what happened

    Use guided questions and the Investigation workspace to record the account in ordinary language.

  3. 03

    Review identified facts and gaps

    Check proposed facts, issues, dates, supporting evidence and information still needed.

  4. 04

    Approve the permanent record

    Select only accurate, reviewed information for publication into the case workspace.

  5. 05

    Continue in McKenzieCMS

    Manage the wider case, documents and published records in the connected case-management workspace.

Clear limits

Practical support, not legal representation.

CourtReady helps organise and prepare information. It does not replace a solicitor or provide legal representation. Users remain responsible for reviewing and approving information before it enters their case record.

May help with

  • Organising case papers and evidence
  • Chronologies, checklists and bundle indexes
  • Statement preparation support
  • Hearing day planning and note-taking
  • Post-hearing action summaries

Does not provide

  • Representation as a solicitor or barrister
  • Guaranteed outcomes
  • Conduct of litigation where not authorised
  • Rights of audience unless the court permits it
  • Emergency safeguarding, counselling or crisis support

FAQ

Common questions.

Is CourtReadySupport.co.uk a solicitors' firm?

No. It provides practical court preparation and McKenzie Friend support. It does not act as a solicitor or barrister.

Can you speak for me in court?

A McKenzie Friend does not automatically have a right to address the court. Any participation beyond quiet support, note-taking and help with papers is for the court to decide.

What should I include in an enquiry?

Include your hearing date or deadline, the court if known, what documents you have and the kind of support you need. Avoid sending highly sensitive documents in a first message.

Do I still need legal advice?

You may need regulated legal advice where legal rights, settlement, child safety, finances, appeals or serious consequences are involved.
