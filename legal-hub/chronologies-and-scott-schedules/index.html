Chronologies and Scott schedules

Two practical tools for organising events, allegations, responses and evidence.

Practical guide CourtReadySupport practical guide

Chronology

  • A chronology is a dated list of important events.
  • Keep entries short and factual.
  • Include document references where the event is supported by evidence.

Scott schedule

  • A Scott schedule is often used to set out specific allegations, responses and evidence in a table.
  • Each row should deal with one allegation or issue.
  • Columns often include date, allegation, response, evidence and court finding.

Practical checks

  • Use neutral wording and avoid repeating the whole case in every row.
  • Make sure dates match your documents.
  • Update the schedule when new directions or evidence change what is in issue.

Important note

The court may require a specific format. Use any template or directions provided in your case.
