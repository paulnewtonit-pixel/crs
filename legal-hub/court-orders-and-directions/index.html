Court basics

Court orders and directions

How to read an order, pick out deadlines, and turn directions into a practical task list.

Practical guide CourtReadySupport practical guide

Start with the order

  • Read the whole order once, then go back and mark every date, action and named person.
  • Look for words such as must, shall, file, serve, disclose, exchange and attend.
  • Separate court deadlines from personal preparation tasks so nothing is hidden in a paragraph.

Build the action list

  • Create one line per direction: what must happen, who must do it, when it is due and where it must be sent.
  • Add reminders several days before the real deadline.
  • Keep proof of sending, including email receipts or postal tracking where relevant.

When something is unclear

  • Do not ignore a direction because it feels confusing.
  • Check whether the order gives permission to contact the court office for administrative clarification.
  • If the issue affects your rights or the case direction, consider legal advice before the deadline passes.

Important note

Court orders can carry consequences if missed. This guide is practical information only, not legal advice.
