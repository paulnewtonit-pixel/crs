Preparing for a court hearing

A calm structure for the days before the hearing, arrival, papers, notes and post-hearing follow-up.

Practical guide CourtReadySupport practical guide

One week before

  • Check the hearing date, time, format, court address or joining details.
  • Review the latest order and confirm what the hearing is listed to decide.
  • Prepare a short list of the points you need the court to understand.

What to take

  • Your bundle or key documents, the latest order, your notes, a chronology and any position statement.
  • A spare copy of essential documents in case the court or another party does not have them.
  • Pen, paper, charger, water and any accessibility information you need to raise.

After the hearing

  • Write down what happened while it is fresh.
  • Check the order when it arrives and compare it with your notes.
  • Turn any new directions into a dated action list immediately.

Important note

If there is immediate danger, call 999. Do not wait for website support.
