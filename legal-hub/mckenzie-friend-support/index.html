Court support

McKenzie Friend support

What practical courtroom support can look like, where the limits sit, and what to prepare before asking the court.

Practical guide CourtReadySupport practical guide

What this means

  • A McKenzie Friend may sit with a litigant in person, help organise papers, take notes and quietly prompt practical points.
  • They do not automatically have a right to speak for you, conduct the case, sign documents or act as a solicitor.
  • The judge remains in control of what support is allowed in the hearing.

Before the hearing

  • Prepare a short note explaining who your supporter is and what help you are asking the court to allow.
  • Keep your papers indexed so the supporter can find the right document quickly.
  • Agree simple signals for note-taking, questions to return to, and anything you must not forget.

CourtReadySupport can help with

  • Preparing a permission note for McKenzie Friend support.
  • Sorting the papers and creating a hearing-day checklist.
  • Helping you understand the practical boundaries before you arrive.

Important note

A McKenzie Friend is not the same as a solicitor or barrister. Ask for regulated legal advice where legal rights, settlement, risk or strategy are involved.
