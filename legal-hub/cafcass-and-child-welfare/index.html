Children

CAFCASS, reports and the welfare checklist

A practical overview of CAFCASS involvement, child reports and welfare checklist preparation.

Practical guide CourtReadySupport practical guide

CAFCASS involvement

  • CAFCASS may carry out safeguarding checks and speak with parties in child arrangements cases.
  • They may provide information or recommendations to help the court decide next steps.
  • Be clear, factual and child-focused when preparing for conversations.

Reports

  • Different reports may look at safeguarding, wishes and feelings, parenting arrangements or specific concerns.
  • Read any report carefully and mark factual points you agree with, disagree with or need to clarify.
  • Connect responses to evidence where possible.

Welfare checklist

  • The court's focus is the child's welfare.
  • Think about needs, safety, relationships, change, harm and each parent's ability to meet needs.
  • Prepare child-focused examples rather than adult conflict points.

Important note

Where a child or adult is at risk of harm, use emergency or specialist safeguarding support.
