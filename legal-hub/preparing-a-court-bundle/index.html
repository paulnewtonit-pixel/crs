Preparing a court bundle

How to turn scattered papers into an indexed, paginated bundle that is easier to use at court.

Practical guide CourtReadySupport practical guide

What a bundle is

  • A bundle is the organised set of documents the court and parties use for a hearing.
  • It should usually be paginated, indexed and arranged in a sensible order.
  • The court order may say who prepares it, what goes in it and when it must be sent.

Practical structure

  • Start with the latest orders, applications and statements.
  • Group evidence by type, date or issue so it can be found quickly.
  • Remove duplicates unless there is a clear reason to keep both copies.

Quality checks

  • Check every page number in the index.
  • Make sure scans are readable and the right way up.
  • Name digital files clearly, using dates and short descriptions.

Important note

Always follow the specific court directions for your case. Bundle rules can differ by court, case type and hearing.
